Dear Committee,

This letter is to inform you that I have submitted my candidature for the 2021 Computer Applications - Computation Arts (BCompSc) program with all of the necessary documentation.

I am currently finishing my DEC in Interactive Media Arts at Dawson College with a current R Score of 33.07. While attending this program I have taken up Calculus 1, Calculus 2 and Linear Algebra (201-NYA, 201-NYB and 201-NYC) on top of my regular schedule to be fully ready for my university program. Despite the additional load, I have excelled with my creativity and my motivation to learn more and push myself further, as seen in my various projects on my website. For example, in my Interactive Media Workshop and The Art of Physical Computing class, I worked on independent projects separate from the rest of my classmates, so that I could better utilize my capabilities and further my knowledge.

Speaking of my portfolio, I have completed a variety of projects in a pretty diverse set of fields (3D visualizations, 2D games, a mobile communication platform, a tiny bit of robotics, some video editing, a music composition). That being said, I'm far from knowing everything that I need or want, so knowing and exploring robotics, real world interactivity projects, combining the procedural world of computers and programming with the real world, integrating inter-connectivity and interactivity with everyday objects and discover the black box that is the field of AI and, among others, machine learning are a few of the interests that I have. Merging these two universes is my main objective in university and in my future career, because I find it not only super interesting, but I also believe it’s the future of our world!

In that frame of mind, I do have a comprehensive knowledge of how to use software in the likes of Blender, Unity, Visual Studio and the .NET environment, HTML, CSS & Js, Processing & p5.js, some of the Adobe Suite as well as a bit of the Affinity suite. In fact, I have been programming from the age of 13, having self-taught myself (with the help of a plethora of YouTube™ tutorials) the majority of everything I know in coding, software and general computing. However, the pursuit of knowledge is never ending and I would love to be accepted into Concordia in my desired program to learn more about ways to deepen my understanding of the software I already know, to learn about its alternative uses, as well as new software, techniques and creative applications.

Truthfully, my trust in Concordia as my university of choice comes from the numerous co-op opportunities, a dynamic learning environment situated on a campus located in an exciting part of the downtown Montréal area, which I hope I'll be able to visit and experience. In the interest of pursuing a career in the field of computation arts and general computing, I can’t express my excitement enough to be able to start learning and collaborating on all kinds of innovative projects during my time at Concordia!

Finally, since I was little and having little grasp of the world, I dreamed of being an inventor. Even if I can't fulfill this childhood dream, I really believe that a program like the one Concordia offers and the job opportunities that will come with it can help me get as close as possible as having a career child me imagined. Through Computer Science - Computation Arts, therefore learning and confronting programming and creative patterns, I'll be able to come up with innovative solutions to today's and tomorrow's problems. If that's not being an inventor, then I don't know what is.

Feel free to reach me through them if you have any additional questions, my email address is stated above.

Thank you for considering my application and have a great day :)

Augusto Mota Pinheiro
